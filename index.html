Module 1 — Introduction & Basic Concepts

What is Kinematics? The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion. Kinematics tells us how objects move (position, velocity, acceleration), not why.

Frame of Reference & Sign Convention

  • Frame of reference: Coordinate system used to measure positions and displacements.
  • Sign convention: Choose positive direction (right/up); keep consistent. For vertical motion, up = positive, g = -10 m/s² if up is positive.

Module 2 — Distance, Displacement, Speed & Velocity

Distance: Scalar, total path length.
Displacement: Vector, straight-line change in position (Δx or s).

Average speed = Total distance / Total time.
Average velocity = Displacement / Time.
Instantaneous velocity v = ds/dt.

Module 3 — Acceleration

Acceleration a = dv/dt. Average acceleration = Δv/Δt. Instantaneous acceleration a = d²s/dt².

Module 4 — Equations of Motion (Derivations)

  1. First equation: v = u + at.
  2. Second equation: s = ut + ½at².
  3. Third equation: v² = u² + 2as.

Module 5 — Graphical Analysis

Slope of x–t = velocity; slope of v–t = acceleration; area under v–t = displacement.

Module 6 & 7 — Relative & Projectile Motion (Summary)

Relative velocity: v_AB = v_A - v_B. Projectile motion: horizontal x = u cosθ · t; vertical y = u sinθ · t - ½ g t²; range, max height, trajectory formulae included in PDF.
